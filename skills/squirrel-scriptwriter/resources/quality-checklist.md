# Quality Checklist — Squirrel Finance Scripts

## Pre-Publish Checklist (7 Core Questions)

From Brand Bible Section 14.2. ALL must pass before publishing.

- [ ] **1. Hook connects to real pain?** — Does the opening 15-20 seconds connect to a real pain point or genuine curiosity the viewer has?
- [ ] **2. Simple example used?** — Is the core concept explained with at least one very simple, easy-to-picture example?
- [ ] **3. No unexplained jargon?** — Is every financial term either translated into squirrel-world metaphor or explained in plain language?
- [ ] **4. Retention beats present?** — Are there at least 3-5 retention beats (visual absurdity, joke, twist, cliffhanger, new example, escalation, mini-payoff)?
- [ ] **5. Tension/downside clear?** — Is the downside, risk, or tension of the topic clearly communicated?
- [ ] **6. Memorable takeaway?** — Is the final takeaway short, sharp, and easy to repeat in comments?
- [ ] **7. Viewer feels smarter?** — Will the viewer honestly feel smarter and clearer after watching?

## Content Gap Compliance (4 Mandatory Questions)

Every script MUST answer all four. From Content Gap Strategy Section 7.

- [ ] **8. What is it?** — The concept is explained simply and clearly
- [ ] **9. How does it work?** — The mechanism is exposed step-by-step
- [ ] **10. Where is this showing up in real life?** — Connected to current reality with data
- [ ] **11. What should ordinary people notice?** — Practical lens, warning signs, or rule of thumb provided

## Script Upgrade Rules (5 Rules)

From Content Gap Strategy Section 9.

- [ ] **12. Hard part included?** — At least 1 section touches on the point where audiences are usually most vague
- [ ] **13. Applied bridge present?** — Contains segments like "This is why your rent feels insane" or "This is why you're feeling it now"
- [ ] **14. Practical lens at end?** — Includes warning sign / pattern to notice / rule of thumb / question to ask
- [ ] **15. Explanation vs opinion separated?** — If controversial assessment present, clearly labeled as interpretation
- [ ] **16. Sequel hook planted?** — End opens up the next video naturally

## Brand Consistency Checks

- [ ] **17. Metaphor consistent?** — Squirrel/acorn/golden leaf system used correctly throughout, no breaks
- [ ] **18. Voice correct?** — Conversational, simple, fast, visual, lightly absurd, never preachy
- [ ] **19. Humor from situations?** — All humor comes from squirrel situations, not audience mockery
- [ ] **20. Not investment advice?** — Script explains, does not recommend specific investments
- [ ] **21. Squirrel = audience?** — Main character is relatable ordinary person, not a guru

## Technical Checks

- [ ] **22. Word count in range?** — 1500-3500 words (8-15 min target)
- [ ] **23. Retention beat frequency?** — At least one beat every 20-40 seconds (~60-130 words)
- [ ] **24. Data points sourced?** — All statistics/numbers are real and from recent sources (2024-2026 preferred)
- [ ] **25. Visual handoff ready?** — Visual DNA Brief captures style lock, character lock, symbol lock, overlay candidates, and the top 5-8 key visual beats for downstream prompt skills

## Scoring

| Score | Quality Level |
|-------|--------------|
| 25/25 | 🟢 Ready to produce |
| 20-24 | 🟡 Good with minor fixes |
| 15-19 | 🟠 Needs revision |
| <15 | 🔴 Major rewrite needed |
