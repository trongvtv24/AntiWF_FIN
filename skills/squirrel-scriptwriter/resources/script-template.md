---

## Outline Structure

### 1. REAL-LIFE HOOK (0:00 - 0:20)
**Goal**: Make viewer feel "This affects me."

- Open with a situation or question tied to real financial pain
- Clear stakes — what the viewer might lose or misunderstand
- An easy-to-picture scenario
- A question, contradiction, or weird outcome

**❌ Do NOT**: "Today we're going to talk about...", dictionary definitions, long historical setups

**Retention beat**: [Absurdity / Twist / Shocking number]

---

### 2. SIMPLIFIED CONCEPT (0:20 - 1:00)
**Goal**: Core idea in 1-2 easy-to-picture lines.

- What is this thing in squirrel-world terms?
- One sentence that makes it click
- Use metaphor: acorns, golden leaves, the forest

**Retention beat**: [Visual metaphor / Mini-payoff]

---

### 3. STEP-BY-STEP MECHANISM (1:00 - 4:00)
**Goal**: Break the system into small causal steps.

- Step A: [Cause → Effect] with real data
- Step B: [Cause → Effect] with real data  
- Step C: [Cause → Effect] with real data
- Step D (optional): [Cause → Effect]

**Must include**: At least 1 "hard part" that most viewers are vague on

**Retention beats** (need 3-5 in this section):
- [ ] Visual absurdity
- [ ] Small joke
- [ ] Twist/surprise
- [ ] New example with real numbers
- [ ] Escalation

---

### 4. WHY PEOPLE GET PULLED IN (4:00 - 5:30)
**Goal**: Explain the psychological appeal.

- Why does this look smart/safe/easy?
- What makes people trust it?
- Where does the confidence come from?

**Retention beat**: [The squirrel acts out the temptation]

---

### 5. THE CATCH (5:30 - 7:00)
**Goal**: Reveal the hidden downside.

- "But here's the catch..."
- Real-world example with specific numbers
- The moment the squirrel realizes something is wrong

**Retention beat**: [Emotional reversal / Shocking stat]

---

### 6. ESCALATION (7:00 - 9:00)
**Goal**: From one squirrel's experience → whole forest.

- What happens when EVERYONE does this?
- System-level consequences
- Historical example if applicable (with numbers)
- "This is why regular people feel the pain first."

**Retention beat**: [Historical story / Scale revelation]

---

### 7. WHY IT MATTERS NOW (9:00 - 11:00)
**Goal**: Reconnect to current real life.

- Current data (2024-2026)
- Applied bridge: "This is why your [grocery bill / rent / savings] feels [broken / stuck / unfair]"
- "This is what it looks like in real life right now"

**Retention beat**: [Current shocking statistic]

---

### 8. TAKEAWAY (11:00 - 12:30)
**Goal**: One sharp mental model the viewer keeps forever.

- "If you only remember one thing, remember this:"
- [The memorable one-liner]
- Practical lens: warning sign / pattern / rule of thumb / question to ask
- NOT investment advice — a framework for thinking

**Format**: Short. Sharp. Memorable. Repeatable in comments.

---

### 9. COMMUNITY CTA (12:30 - 13:00)
**Goal**: Invite engagement + plant sequel hook.

- Sequel hook: "The next layer of this story is..."
- Question for comments: "What money topic still confuses you?"
- Offer 2 specific next-topic options
- "See you in the forest."

## Retention Beat Types (Reference)

| Beat Type | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Visual absurdity | Squirrel doing something ridiculous | "The squirrel drowning in golden leaves" |
| Small joke | Situational humor from the metaphor | "Very smart. Very sustainable. Definitely not how disasters begin." |
| Twist | Expectation reversal | "Sounds great, right? Well..." |
| Mini cliffhanger | Tease what's coming | "And this is where things get weird." |
| New example | Fresh concrete number/story | "In 2024, OpenAI was valued at $840 billion." |
| Escalation | Scale up from 1 squirrel to forest | "Now imagine every squirrel doing this at once." |
| Mini-payoff | Small "aha" moment | "That's why saving alone can feel broken." |
