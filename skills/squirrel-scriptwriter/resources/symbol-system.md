# Symbol System — Squirrel Finance Metaphor Mapping

## Core Symbols

| Real World | Squirrel World | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Money / Cash / USD | **Golden leaves** | Always plural. The universal currency of the forest. |
| Assets / Goods / Value | **Acorns** | Anything of real, tangible value. Can be consumed or planted. |
| Long-term growth / Wealth-building | **Oak tree** | What happens when acorns are planted and given time. |
| Home / Safety / Security | **Nest** | The squirrel's personal shelter. Emotional anchor. |
| Banks / Lenders / Credit | **Forest Bank** | Where squirrels borrow and deposit. Can be helpful or predatory. |
| The Economy / Society / Markets | **The Forest** | The entire ecosystem. Always in motion. |
| Recession / Tightening | **Winter** | Harsh, scary, but cyclical. Squirrels must prepare. |
| Crisis / Collapse / Shocks | **Storm / Wildfire / Food shortage** | Sudden, destructive. Beyond individual control. |
| People / Institutions / Governments | **Other animals** | Foxes, raccoons, bears, owls — used for various roles. |
| The Viewer / Ordinary Person | **The Squirrel (main character)** | Always relatable. Never the expert. |

## Financial Concepts Mapped

| Concept | Squirrel Translation | Example Usage |
|---------|---------------------|---------------|
| Interest | Extra golden leaves owed over time | "The Forest Bank says: bring back more leaves than you borrowed" |
| Leverage | Borrowing more leaves to buy more acorns | "Using borrowed leaves to chase bigger acorn piles" |
| Inflation | Same leaves buy fewer acorns | "The pile looks the same, but the forest got more expensive" |
| Stocks | Owning part of an oak tree or acorn orchard | "You don't own the whole tree, just a branch" |
| Bonds | Lending leaves to the forest in exchange for future leaves | "The forest borrows your leaves and promises to return more later" |
| Dividends | Acorns that fall from a tree you partly own | "Your branch drops acorns into your nest periodically" |
| Bubbles | Buying acorns not for need but hoping another squirrel pays more | "Nobody asks what the acorn does. They ask how high it goes." |
| Crashes | The whole forest panic-selling at once | "Every squirrel is running for the exit at the same time" |
| Taxes | Some leaves taken to maintain the forest | "Part of your pile goes to keeping the trails clean" |
| Compound growth | Acorn sprout → sapling → tree, each layer growing on the last | "The tree starts helping. Growth builds on growth." |
| Diversification | Planting different types of acorns in different soil | "Don't put all your acorns in one hole" |
| Dollar-cost averaging | Buying acorns regularly, same amount of leaves each time | "Every month, same leaves, buy acorns. No prophecy needed." |
| Credit cards | Borrowing leaves now, paying back more leaves later | "Spend now, owe later — with extra leaves on top" |
| Mortgage | Borrowing a huge pile of leaves to buy a nest | "The Forest Bank owns most of your nest until you pay it off" |
| ETF / Index fund | Owning a tiny piece of every oak tree in the forest | "Instead of picking one tree, you own a sliver of the whole forest" |
| Fees | Small percentage taken from your acorn pile regularly | "A tiny bite every year that eats future branches" |
| FOMO | Fear of being the only squirrel not at the acorn party | "Everyone else seems rich. You feel stupid sitting out." |
| Risk | The chance your acorn never becomes an oak tree | "Not every acorn grows. Some just sit there. Some get eaten." |
| Emergency fund | Extra leaves kept in the nest for surprise storms | "Leaves for the kind of week where everything breaks" |
| Hyperinflation | Leaves become worthless, need a wheelbarrow for one acorn | "The forest has to start over with something new" |
| Central bank policy | The Forest Bank's big decisions about how many leaves exist | "The Bank decides: more leaves or fewer leaves?" |
| Supply chain | The path from seed → tree → acorn → market | "If the trail gets blocked, acorns stop arriving" |
| Speculation | Buying because you think the price will go up, not because you need it | "Buying the story, not the acorn" |
| Debt trap | Borrowing to pay back borrowing | "Borrowing leaves to pay back the leaves you already borrowed" |

## Emotion-to-Visual Mapping

| Squirrel Emotion | When to Use | Visual Cue |
|-----------------|------------|------------|
| Happy/excited | Good news, early gains, discovery | Big smile, holding acorn up |
| Shocked/betrayed | Price reveals, inflation reality | Wide eyes, pointing at text |
| Confused | Complex concepts being introduced | Head tilted, question marks |
| Frustrated/annoyed | Hidden costs, fees, losing power | Crossed arms, frowning |
| Panicked/running | Crashes, hyperinflation, crises | Running, sweat drops |
| Defeated/exhausted | Worst-case scenarios | Sitting on floor, slumped |
| Wise/teaching | Explaining mechanisms | Pointer stick, chalkboard |
| Proud/confident | Takeaway moments, smart moves | Standing tall, cape/crown |
| Greedy/hypnotized | FOMO, bubble mania | Heart eyes, grabbing acorns |
| Sad/worried | Savers getting hurt, real pain | Holding cracked piggy bank |
